Creating a custom ancestry

1. Think of your background

Your first step in creating a character is to consider your character's species, ancestry, the culture they grew up in, and any exceptional occurrences that may have shaped them. As you make choices about your character’s features, think up a few key characteristics of your character’s past.

  • Where did they spend most of their time?
  • What did they do for a living?
  • What capabilities and possessions did they acquire?
  • What language did they learn from their family, associates, or studies?
  • How did their past affect their physical abilities?

Start by determining what you look like and what species make up your unique ancestry. You can choose a premade species from the list below, or use this resource to build your own. The species available to you in your game are determined by your DM. If you use this resource, you choose which ability scores you are proficient in, your size, if you have darkvision, individuals representing your ancestry, cultures representing your shaping influences, and what languages you know.

Once you have a character in mind, follow these steps in order, making decisions that reflect your character best. Your character will likely evolve with each choice you make. What’s important is that you come to the table with a character you’re excited to play.

Pick a premade ancestry…

The simplest option is to pick a an existing ancestry. Or, you can work with your DM who has some prebuilt stat blocks for different species that exist in this world. Each species has various ancestries and a recommended set of traits to use for your character. If you choose a premade build, skip to step 9.

2. Determine your creature type

Every creature in D&D, including every player character, has a special tag in the rules that identifies the type of creature they are. Most player characters are of the Humanoid type. Unless you and your DM decide otherwise, your character’s creature type is Humanoid.

The game’s creature types include: Aberration, Beast, Celestial, Construct, Dragon, Elemental, Fey, Fiend, Giant, Humanoid, Monstrosity, Ooze, Plant, Undead. These types don’t have rules themselves, but some rules in the game affect creatures of certain types in different ways. For example, the text of the cure wounds spell specifies that the spell doesn’t work on a creature of the Construct type.

3. Choose your size

Most species have individuals that vary in size, but tend to have an average that most fit into. Some species are only a certain size, such as goliaths (medium) or gnomes (small). Choose one of the following options:

Medium

Medium creatures vary widely in height and build, from barely 5 feet to over 8 feet tall and from nearly 100 to over 400 pounds.

Small

Small creatures are short and light, often shorter than 4 feet tall and less than 80 pounds.

5. Can you see in the dark

Not all species have the ability to naturally see in the dark. Choose if your ancestry grants you this ability.

6. Determine spellcasting ability

Your heritage may have gifted you the ability to cast certain spells. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for any spell gained in this way. If the class you choose for your character cannot cast spells, you may choose Constitution as your heritage spellcasting ability instead.

You may take at most one 1st-level and one 2nd-level spell through traits. 1st-level spells become available at level 3, and 2nd-level spells at level 5. Any spells taken through a trait do not take a material component to cast. Once you cast a spell from a trait, you can’t cast that spell again until you finish a long rest. You can also cast these spells using any spell slots you have of the appropriate spell level.

The “spells” you take through this ability may actually be spell-like properties of your ancestry rather than a magical spell. For example, a reptilian character’s acid splash may represent their venomous spit.

7. Choose your ancestries

You can choose up to 2 ancestries from the following options to make up your heritage. The traits you choose represent the physical characteristics and skills of your character. If you are customizing an existing Ancestry Archetype, you may be required to pick certain ancestry traits from the options below.

Planar ancestries

Elemental

You have the blood of an elemental, such as a genie, in your ancestry, or have been altered by elemental energy.

Elemental Attunement (required). If you have elemental ancestry, you must take this trait. Choose one of the following types:

  • Air (2). You gain resistance to lightning or thunder damage. If you have darkvision, your darkvision is tinged with wisps of white. You speak primordial (auran).
  • Earth (2). You know the stone forming or manipulate earth cantrips, or the blade ward cantrip which can be cast normally or as a bonus action a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest. If you have darkvision you see in a shade of your choice. You speak primordial (terran).
  • Fire (3). You gain resistance to fire damage. If you have darkvision you see in shades of red. You speak primordial (ignan).
  • Nature (2). You gain resistance to acid or poison damage. If you have darkvision you see in shades of brown or green. You speak primordial (giant).
  • Water (2). You gain resistance to cold damage. If you have darkvision you see in shades of blue. You speak primordial (aquan).

Unending Breath (1). You can hold your breath indefinitely while you’re not incapacitated.

Cantrip (2). You know one cantrip of your choice associated with your elemental attunement chosen from the list below:

  • Air. Gust, lightning lure, shocking grasp, wind lash, zap.
  • Earth. Blade ward, mold earth, magic stone, resistance.
  • Fire. Control flames, create bonfire, light, produce flame.
  • Nature. Acid splash, infestation, druidcraft, poison spray, primal savagery.
  • Water. Acid splash, shape water, frostbite, ray of frost, water lash.

Elemental Strike (3). When you damage a creature with an attack, you can cause the attack to deal extra damage of your elemental attunement type. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

  • Air. 1d4 + your Proficiency Bonus lightning or thunder damage and the target has disadvantage to maintain Concentration on a spell.
  • Earth. Knock a Large or smaller creature prone. The target makes an Athletics or Acrobatics save to avoid.
  • Fire. 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus fire damage.
  • Nature. Your Proficiency Bonus acid or poison damage, and give disadvantage to the next ability check or saving throw the target makes until the start of your next turn.
  • Water. 1d4 + your Proficiency Bonus acid or cold damage, and reduce the target’s Speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn.

Elemental Magic (7). Starting at 3rd level, you can cast one spell associated with your elemental attunement chosen from the list below.

  • Air. Chromatic orb (lightning or thunder only), feather fall, gust of wind, jump, longstrider, thunderous smite, thunderwave, zephyr strike.
  • Earth. Absorb elements, earth tremor, expeditious retreat, longstrider, shield of faith (self only).
  • Fire. Burning hands, chromatic orb (fire only), heroism, purify food and drink, sanctuary, wrathful smite.
  • Nature. Caustic brew, chromatic orb (acid only), ensnaring strike, entangle, hail of thorns.
  • Water. Armor of arcane ice, chromatic orb (cold only), create or destroy water, fog cloud, frost fingers, ice knife, sanctuary.

Then starting at 5th level, you can cast one spell associated with your elemental attunement chosen from the list below.

  • Air. Blur, gust of wind, levitate, warding wind.
  • Earth. Dust devil, earthbind, earthen grasp, heat metal.
  • Fire. Arcane scorcher, flame blade, pyrotechnics, scorching ray.
  • Nature. Pass without trace, spike growth, wither and bloom.
  • Water. Acid arrow, binding ice, dancing wave, water walk.

Fey

You have the blood of a fey in your ancestry, such as through elvish or gnomish heritage, or you have been otherwise touched by the fey.

Fey (1). You are a fey creature.

Trance (2). You don’t need to sleep, and magic can’t put you to sleep. You can finish a long rest in 4 hours if you spend those hours in a trance-like meditation, during which you retain consciousness. Whenever you finish this trance, you can mystically gain two proficiencies that you don’t have, each one with a weapon or a tool of your choice, and you retain them until you finish your next long rest.

Charm Resistance (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed condition on yourself.

Cantrip (2). You know the dancing lights, druidcraft, friends, minor illusion, or vicious mockery cantrip.

Fey Magic (2). Starting at 3rd level, you can cast one spell from the following list: Animal friendship, cause fear, charm person, color spray, enlarge/reduce, faerie fire, hideous laughter, sleep.

Greater Fey Magic (4). Starting at 5th level, you can cast one spell from the following list: alter self, crown of madness, disorient, invisibility, mischief, suggestion.

Fey Step (6). As a bonus action, you can magically teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Fey Step Improvement (2). Starting at 3rd level, your Fey Step gains one of the following traits, which can be changed after a long rest. The DC equals 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Spellcasting Ability modifier. Requires Fey Step.

  • Autumn. Immediately after you teleport, up to two creatures that you can see within 10 feet of you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be charmed by you for 1 minute, or until you or your companions deal any damage to the creatures.
  • Winter. Before you teleport, one creature of your choice you can see within 5 feet of you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of you until the end of your next turn.
  • Spring. You can touch one willing creature within 5 feet of you. That creature teleports instead of you.
  • Summer. Immediately after you teleport, each creature of your choice that you can see within 5 feet of you takes damage equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

Magic Resistance (6). You have advantage on all Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma saving throws against magic.

Shapechanger (7). As an action you can change your appearance and voice. You determine the specifics of the changes, including your hair length and sex. You also adjust your height and weight and can change your size between Medium and Small. You can make yourself appear as another ancestry, and you must adopt a form that has the same basic arrangement of limbs that you have. Your clothing and equipment aren’t changed by this trait. You stay into your new form until you use an action to revert to your true form or until you die.

Greater Magic Resistance (9). You have advantage on all saving throws against magic.

Planetouched

There is an immortal being in your ancestry, such as a celestial being or fiend, or you have been blessed or cursed by the gods.

Planar Attunement (required). If you have planetouched ancestry, you must take this trait. Choose one of the following attunements:

  • Celestial. Your innate spellcasting draws from the cleric spell list. You are resistant to the following damage type:
    • Radiant (2).
    • Necrotic (3).
  • Fiend. Your innate spellcasting draws from the warlock spell list. You are resistant to the following damage type:
    • Cold (2).
    • Fire (3).
    • Lightning (2).

Cantrip (2). You know one cantrip from the spell list matching your planar attunement.

Planar Magic (2). Starting at 3rd level, you may cast one 1st-level spell chosen from the spell list matching your planar attunement.

Greater Planar Magic (4). Starting at 5th level, you may cast one 2nd-level spell chosen from the spell list matching your planar attunement.

Planar Power (2). Damage that you deal matching your attunement type ignores resistances. If a creature is immune to that damage type, it is resistant and takes half damage instead.

  • Celestial. You cause it to regain hit points equal to 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus.
  • Fiend. You cause it to gain temporary hit points equal to 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus.

Plane Strike (3). When you damage a creature with an attack, you can cause the attack to deal extra damage of your planar attunement type equal to 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Celestial Revelation (3). When you reach 3rd level, you can use a reaction to unleash the celestial energy inside yourself. The benefits of that transformation last for 1 minute or until you end it as a bonus action. Once you transform you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest. Only available for celestial planar attunements. Choose one:

  • Necrotic Shroud. Creatures of your choice within 10 feet of you that can see you must succeed against a Charisma Saving Throw (DC 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Charisma modifier) or become frightened of you until the end of your next turn.
  • Radiant Consumption. Searing light temporarily radiates from your eyes and mouth. For the duration, you shed bright light in a 10-foot radius and dim light an additional 10 feet, and at the end of each of your turns each creature within 10 feet of you make a Constitution saving throw of become blinded until the end of your next turn.
  • Radiant Wings. Spectral wings sprout from your back. Until the transformation ends, you have a flying speed equal to your walking speed.

Shadowborn

A creature from the Shadowfell or other dark places is in your ancestry, or you have been otherwise touched by dark magic.

Brave (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the frightened condition on yourself.

Cantrip (2). You know the chill touch, deathworking, minor illusion, or primal savagery cantrip.

Shadow Magic (2). Beginning at 3rd level, you can cast one 1st-level spell of your choice from the illusion or necromancy schools of magic.

Necrotic Resistance (3). You have resistance to necrotic damage.

Greater Shadow Magic (4). Beginning at 5th level, you can cast one 2nd-level spell of your choice from the illusion or necromancy schools of magic.

Close to Death (4). You have advantage on death saving throws.

Camouflage (4). When you make a Dexterity (Stealth) check, you make the check with advantage, and can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Shifting (7). As a bonus action, you can assume a more bestial appearance for 1 minute, until you die, or until you revert to your normal form as a bonus action. You can shift a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest . When you shift, you gain temporary hit points equal to 2 × your Proficiency Bonus, and an additional benefit chosen when you select this race:

  • Beasthide. You gain 1d6 additional temporary hit points. While shifted, you have a +1 bonus to your Armor Class.
  • Swiftstride. While shifted, your walking speed increases by 10 feet. Additionally, you can move up to 10 feet as a reaction when a creature ends its turn within 5 feet of you. This reactive movement doesn’t provoke opportunity attacks.
  • Wildhunt. While shifted, you have advantage on Wisdom checks, and no creature within 30 feet of you can make an attack roll with advantage against you unless you’re incapacitated.

Step of Darkness (8). Briefly disappearing into the shadows, you magically teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space that you can see. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest. Starting at 3rd level, you gain resistance to all damage when you teleport using this spell. The resistance lasts until the start of your next turn. During that time, you appear ghostly and translucent.

Bestial ancestries

Aquatic

You are descended from an aquatic creature such as a sea elf or triton, or otherwise have traits from an aquatic creature like a fish, shark, salamander, crab, or frog.

Bite or Pincers (1). You can use your toothy maw or chitinous pincers to make unarmed strikes as a light weapon. When you hit with them, the strike deals:

  • Bite. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage
  • Chitinous Pincers. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage

Emissary of the Sea (1). You can communicate simple ideas to any Beast, Elemental, or Monstrosity that has a swimming speed. They can understand the meaning of your words, though you have no special ability to understand them in return.

Amphibious (2). You can breathe both air and water.

Aquatic Resistance (2). You have resistance to one of the following damage types: acid or cold.

Cantrip (2). You know the shape water or acid splash cantrip.

Swim Speed (2). You have a swim speed of 30 feet provided you are not wearing medium or heavy armor.

Defensive Feature (4). When another creature grapples you or starts its turn grappling you, you can choose to use defensive features on your body to damage it. You deal damage equal to 1d4 × your Proficiency Bonus of the damage type chosen.

  • Poisonous. Poison
  • Spines. Piercing

Carapace / Shell (5). Due to your protective exoskeleton, scales, or shell and the shape of your body, you are ill-suited to wearing armor. Your body provides ample protection, however; it gives you a base AC of 17 (your Dexterity modifier doesn’t affect this number). You gain no benefit from wearing armor, but if you are using a shield, you can apply the shield’s bonus as normal. At level 12, the base AC increases to 18 due to your combat experience.

Flying

You have the blood of a flying creature like an aarakocra in your ancestry, and traits (e.g. birdlike, mothlike, batlike) that stem from that ancestry.

Beak and Claw (1). You have a beak and/or talons that allow you to make damaging unarmed strikes as a light weapon. When you hit with it, the strike deals:

  • Talons. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage
  • Beak. 1d6 + Strength modifier piercing damage

Cantrip (2). You can control wind and know the gust cantrip, or your voice carries on the wind and you know the message cantrip.

Keen Sight (6). You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception), Wisdom (Survival), and Intelligence (Investigation) checks that rely on sight.

Flight. You have vestigial wings. When you fall at least 10 feet and aren’t incapacitated, you can use your Reaction to safely glide downward, taking no damage from the fall. In addition, when you take the Jump Action, you can flap your wings to gain Advantage on that Action’s Ability Check. Choose one:

  • Glide (3). You can move up to 2 feet horizontally for every 1 foot you descend.
  • Limited Flight (5). At 1st level, you can use your bonus action to gain a flying speed equal to your walking speed for one turn, after which time you fall to the ground. At 5th level, this ability increases to a flying speed equal to your walking speed for ten minutes. This ability is tiring, and can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.
  • Wings (9). You have a flying speed equal to your walking speed.

To use this ability, you can’t be wearing medium or heavy armor.

Mammalian

A mammalian creature is in your ancestry, such as a tabaxi or loxodon, or you otherwise have physical traits of a mammal like a cat, dog, elephant, hippo, gorilla, or antelope. The features you choose below must match your physical features. For example, a cat’s tail can’t hold a weapon.

Tooth and Nail (1). You have one or more features that allow you to make damaging unarmed strikes as a natural, light weapon. When you hit with it, the strike deals:

  • Bite. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage
  • Claws. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage
  • Hooves. 1d6 + Strength modifier bludgeoning damage
  • Horns. 1d6 + Strength modifier piercing or bludgeoning damage (choose one)
  • Natural strength. 1d6 + Strength modifier bludgeoning damage

Traversal (1). You can move across difficult terrain without expending extra movement if you are using your walking speed and are on the ground or floor.

Climb Speed (2). You have a climbing speed of 30 feet.

Leap (2). Your long jump is 20 feet and your high jump is 10 feet, with or without a running start.

Roar (2). As a bonus action, you can let out an especially menacing roar. Creatures of your choice within 10 feet of you that can hear you must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become frightened of you until the end of your next turn. The DC of the save equals 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Constitution modifier. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Agile Fall (3). When you fall 100 feet or less and aren't incapacitated, you can make a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check when you land. If you succeed, you take no falling damage and land on your feet.

Prehensile Limb. You have a prehensile limb such as a tail and feet with opposable thumbs, an extra set of limbs, or a trunk that can perform simple tasks: lift, drop, hold, push, or pull an object or a creature; open or close a door or a container; grapple someone; or make an unarmed strike (damage dice of 1d4) with a reach of 5 feet. The limb(s) can use an object as a bonus action, but cannot wield a shield or perform somatic components of a spell.

  • Appendage (3). You can hold an extra weapon with the light property.
  • Trunk (2). Your trunk can act as a snorkel, and can lift a number of pounds equal to five times your Strength score.

Keen Smell (3). Thanks to your keen sense of smell, you have advantage on Wisdom (Perception), Wisdom (Survival), and Intelligence (Investigation) checks that involve smell.

  • Hunter’s Senses (+4) As an action you can focus your sense of smell and gain blindsight (30 ft.) until the end of your next turn.

Burrow (5). At 1st level, you can use your bonus action to gain a burrow speed of 10 feet for one turn. At 5th level, your burrow speed increases to 20 feet.

Swiftness (5). When you move on your turn in combat, you can double your speed until the end of the turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.

Pack Tactics (8). You have advantage on an attack roll against a creature if at least one of your allies is within 5 feet of the creature and the ally isn’t incapacitated.

Reptilian

You are descended from reptilian creature such as a lizardfolk, tortle, or yuan-ti, or otherwise have reptilian traits, and were hatched from an egg.

Hold Breath (1). You can hold your breath for up to 1 hour at a time.

Tooth and Tail (1). Your toothy maw and/or strong tail allow you to make damaging unarmed strikes as a light weapon. When you hit with it, the strike deals:

  • Bite. 1d6 + Strength modifier slashing damage
  • Fangs. 1d6 + Strength modifier piercing damage
  • Tail. 1d6 + Strength modifier bludgeoning damage

Shell Defense (1). You have a shell and can withdraw into it as an action. Until you emerge, you gain a +4 bonus to AC, and you have advantage on Strength and Constitution saving throws. While in your shell, you are treated as being prone, your speed is 0 and can’t increase, you have disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws, you can’t take reactions, attack rolls made against you do not have advantage normally granted by the prone condition, and the only action you can take is a bonus action to emerge from your shell.

Hungry Jaws (3). As a bonus action, you can make a special attack with your bite. If the attack hits, it deals normal damage, and you gain temporary hit points (minimum of 1) equal to your Proficiency Bonus. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Natural Armor (3). You can calculate your AC as 13 + your Dexterity modifier. A shield’s benefits apply as normal while you use your natural armor.

Camouflage (4). When you make a Dexterity (Stealth) check, you make the check with advantage, and can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Regeneration (4). As a bonus action, you can heal wounds you have taken. You may spend a hit die to regain that much health a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Chromatophore (6). As a bonus action, you can turn invisible until the start of your next turn. This effect ends early if you make an attack roll or force a creature to make a saving throw. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Other Physiologies

Construct

You have a constructed nature. You may have been an organic creature that has been partially replaced by machinery, or a wholly unique construct with a specific purpose.

Toxin Resilience (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the poisoned or diseased conditions on yourself.

Vigilant Rest (2). When you take a long rest, you can spend at least six hours in an inactive, motionless state, rather than sleeping. In this state, you appear inert, but it doesn’t render you unconscious, and you can see and hear as normal.

Natural Armor (3). You can calculate your AC as 13 + your Dexterity modifier. A shield’s benefits apply as normal while you use your natural armor.

Poison Resistance (3). You are resistant to poison damage.

Constructed Form (4). You are a Construct. You don’t need to eat, drink, or breathe, and magic can’t put you to sleep. You are immune to disease and have advantage on saving throws against being paralyzed.

Built for Success (4). You can add a d4 to one attack roll, ability check, or saving throw you make after seeing the d20 roll but before the effects of the roll are resolved. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Healing Machine (4). If the mending spell is cast on you, you can spend a Hit Die, roll it, and regain a number of hit points equal to the roll plus your Constitution modifier (minimum of 1 hit point). In addition, your creator designed you to benefit from several spells that preserve life but that normally don't affect constructs: cure wounds, healing word, mass cure wounds, mass healing word, and spare the dying.

Integrated Protection (6). You gain a +1 bonus to Armor Class. Donning and doffing armor other than a shield takes 1 hour, is integrated into and cannot be removed from your body while you live, and can be done while you rest.

Draconic

You are descended from a dragon. Only available for dragonborn species.

Draconic Ancestry (required). If you have draconic ancestry, you must take this trait. Choose one of the following damage types:

  • (0) Acid, cold, force, lightning, radiant, thunder.
  • (1) Fire, necrotic, poison, psychic.

Cantrip (2). You know one cantrip of your choice from the sorcerer spell list.

Draconic Resistance (2). You have resistance to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry.

Draconic Warding (4). Starting at 5th level, as an action, you can channel your draconic energy to protect yourself. For 1 minute, you become immune to the damage type associated with your draconic ancestry. You can’t use this trait again until you finish a long rest.

Draconic Presence (4). You have advantage on Charisma (Intimidation) checks.

Draconic Cry (5). As a bonus action, you let out a cry at your enemies within 10 feet of you. Until the start of your next turn, you and your allies have advantage on attack rolls against any of those creatures who could hear you. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Breath Weapon (6). When you take the Attack action, you can replace one of your attacks with an exhalation of a magical energy in a 15-foot cone. Each creature in the area must make a Dexterity saving throw of DC 8 + your Constitution modifier + your Proficiency Bonus.

A creature takes 1d10 damage of the type associated with your Draconic Ancestry on a failed save, and half as much damage on a successful one. The damage increases to 2d10 at 5th level, 3d10 at 11th level, and 4d10 at 17th level. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Psionic

You have an innate knack for psionics, having a psionic in your ancestry or having been twisted by the far realm.

Cantrip (2). You know the encode thoughts, mage hand, message, mind sliver, or thaumaturgy cantrip. If you choose mage hand, the hand is invisible and doesn't require components when you cast it with this trait.

Psionic Resistance. You have resistance to one of the following damage types:

  • Force (2).
  • Psychic (3).

Psionic Magic (2). Starting at 3rd level, you can cast one 1st-level spell from the following spell list: bane, catapult, floating disk, hellish rebuke, jump, magnify gravity, shield, unseen servant, wrathful smite.

Greater Psionic Magic (4). Starting at 5th level, you can cast one 2nd-level spell from the following spell list: detect thoughts, kinetic jaunt, levitate, mind spike, mind whip, phantasmal force.

Psychic Resistance (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the charmed or  frightened conditions on yourself.

Telepathy (5). You can speak telepathically to any creature within 30 feet of you. The creature understands you only if the two of you share a language. You can speak telepathically in this way to one creature at a time.

Stout

You are exceptionally sturdy, descended from particularly hardy ancestors or species.

Hardy (1). You are acclimated to extreme climates and do not suffer the effects of one of the following:

  • Extreme cold below 0 degrees Fahrenheit
  • Extreme heat above 100 degrees Fahrenheit
  • High altitudes above 10,000 ft.
  • Deep waters below 100 ft (n/a if you have swim speed)

Toxin Resilience (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the poisoned or diseased conditions on yourself.

Poison Resistance (3). You are resistant to poison damage.

Natural Armor (3). You have thick skin. Your AC is 12 + your Constitution modifier. A shield’s benefits apply as normal while you use your natural armor.

Sturdy. Choose one:

  • Relentless (4). When you are reduced to 0 hit points but not killed outright, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You can’t use this feature again until you finish a long rest.
  • Toughness (5). Your hit point maximum increases by 1, and it increases by 1 every time you gain a level.
  • Durable (7). When you take damage, you can use your reaction to roll 1d12 + your Constitution modifier and reduce the damage by that total. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Strong (5). You have advantage on Strength-based ability checks and Strength saving throws.

Endurance (6) (Human only). Your body is naturally able to travel great distances and endure great hardships. If you have no Exhaustion you can use this trait to avoid gaining a level of Exhaustion, regaining its use on a long rest.

8. Choose cultural traits

You can choose traits from up to 2 cultures from the following options to make up your heritage. You must take at least one cultural trait that costs one or more (1+) heritage points.

Artisan

You come from a culture that values the art of craftsmanship.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Artisan's Lore (0). Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) check related to the origin or history of an item related to an artisan tool with which you are proficient, you are considered proficient in the History skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.
  • Craftsman's Memory (1). You can perfectly recall any item you have ever seen or handled, and can describe it in detail, including any flaws or unique features.
  • Tinkerer (2). Whenever you create an item, you can add a small clockwork effect to it. This effect can be a minor light source, sound effect, movement, or visual effect. These effects grant no mechanical benefit or additional value to the item.

Tool Proficiency (1). You gain proficiency with the artisan's tools of your choice.

Cantrip (2). You know the mending, dismantle, or guidance cantrip.

Gifted Artisan (2). When you make an ability check using an artisan's tool with which you are proficient, you can roll 1d4 and add the number rolled to the ability check.

Skilled Artistry (2). The time it takes to craft something using an artisan’s tool with which you are proficient is halved.

Skill Proficiency (2). You gain proficiency with one of the following skills of your choice: Arcana, History, Insight, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand.

Quick Learner (4). You gain one skill proficiency of your choice and one tool proficiency of your choice. Additionally, it takes you half as much time to learn a new language, or train and become proficient in a tool, weapon, armor, or skill.

Friendly

You come from a hospitable culture with an open heart.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Entertainer's Lore (0). Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) or Wisdom (Insight) check related to a gaming set or musical instrument with which you are proficient, you are considered proficient and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.
  • Socializer (0). The first time you interact with strangers in an unfamiliar land, culture, or region, add 1d4 to the first Charisma (Persuasion or Deception) check you make against them. This trait does not work on groups if at least one person in the group knows you.
  • Empathic (1). You have advantage on Wisdom (Insight) checks made to discern the emotions of others.
  • Peaceful Negotiator (1). You have proficiency in the Persuasion skill. When you make a Persuasion check to resolve a conflict without violence, you can roll with advantage.

Tool Proficiency (1). You gain proficiency with one type of gaming set, musical instrument of your choice, a Cook's Utensils, or Herbalism Kit.

Gifted Entertainer (1). When you make an ability check using a gaming set or musical instrument with which you are proficient, you can roll one a d4 and add the number rolled to the ability check.

Skill Proficiency (2). You gain proficiency with one of the following skills of your choice: Acrobatics, Insight, Medicine, Performance, Persuasion.

Healer's Intuition (3). When you make a Wisdom (Medicine) check or an ability check using an Herbalism Kit, you can roll with advantage.

Lucky (4). When you roll a 1 on an attack roll, ability check, or saving throw, you can reroll the die and must use the new roll.

Mage

Arcane magic is common in the society you came from.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one or both:

  • Arcane Training (0). Whenever you make an Intelligence (Arcana) check or an Intelligence (History) check about famous magic users, or the use, application, or casting of spells, you are considered proficient in that skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.
  • Advanced Arcane Training (2). Through study and practice, you are able to scribe scrolls or create potions in half the time, and you have advantage on Intelligence (Arcana) checks to discern the use of spells and enchantments.

Cantrip (2). You learn one cantrip of your choice from the wizard spell list.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Arcana, History, Investigation, Nature, Religion.

Arcane Infusion (3). During a long rest, you can infuse a non-magical item with a cantrip from the wizard spell list. You can maintain a number of these items equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

Destructive Spell (3). When you damage a creature with a spell, you can cause the spell to deal extra damage equal to 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus to a single target of the spell. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Learned Magic (3). Choose one 1st-level spell from the wizard spell list.

Skirmisher

You come from a background specializing in combat and ambush tactics.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Combat Awareness (0). If you are surprised in battle, enemies do not have advantage against you in the surprise round.
  • Trap Master (1). You have a preternatural ability to avoid traps and have advantage on Dexterity or Strength saves against triggered traps.

Finesse Training (2). Your training allows you to add the finesse property to a melee weapon you carry in your main hand. The weapon cannot be a hammer, mace, or have the heavy or two-handed properties.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Athletics, Insight, Nature, Perception, Stealth, or Survival.

Lethal Strike (3). When you damage a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal extra damage equal to 1d4 per point of your Proficiency Bonus. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Skilled Combatant. Choose one:

  • Adrenaline Rush (3). You can take the Dash action as a bonus action. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest. Whenever you use this trait, gain a number of temporary hit points equal to your Proficiency Bonus.
  • Charge (3). Immediately after you use the Dash action on your turn and move at least 20 feet, you can make one unarmed strike melee attack as a bonus action, or you can ram a target and attempt to knock it prone. The DC for this Strength saving throw is equal to 8 + your Strength modifier + your Proficiency Bonus.
  • Ambush (5). If you hit a creature with an attack roll, the creature takes an extra 2d6 damage if it hasn’t taken a turn yet in the current combat.
  • Nimble Escape (7). You can take the Disengage or Hide action as a bonus action on each of your turns.

Streetwise

You grew up in a rough environment or living outside the bounds of the law.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Bamboozler (1). You do something bizarre or unexpected that distracts your opponent. Once per day as a bonus action, you can distract an enemy within 10 feet of you. Add your proficiency bonus to all attack rolls made against the target by creatures that can see and hear you until the start of your next turn. This ability resets at dawn.
  • Improviser (1). You are proficient with improvised weapons, and improvised weapons you use deal 1d6 + Dexterity or Strength modifier damage rather than the damage they normally deal, at the DM’s discretion.
  • Expert Forgery (1). You can duplicate other creatures’ handwriting and craftwork. You have advantage on all checks made to produce forgeries or duplicates of existing objects.

Cantrip (2). You know one cantrip from the bard spell list.

Tool Proficiency (2). You gain proficiency with a gaming set or one set of tools of your choice: thieves' tools, disguise kit, forgery kit, poisoner's kit.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Acrobatics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Performance, Sleight of Hand, or Stealth.

Merge with Shadows (4). Starting at 3rd level, you gain the ability to use pass without trace, treating it as a spell-like ability.

Out of Sight (1). You can attempt to hide even when you are only lightly obscured by foliage, rocky terrain, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, and other natural phenomena, and have advantage on these Dexterity checks.

Thief's Intuition (4). When you make an ability check using thieves' tools, you can roll a d4 and add the number rolled to the ability check.

Skilled (6). When you make an ability check using a skill in which you have proficiency, you can give yourself advantage on the check before rolling a d20. You can give yourself advantage in this way a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest. You cannot take this trait as a Rogue.

Survivalist / Nomad

You come from a frontier culture, skilled in survival and woodcraft.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Beast Tracker (1). Whenever you make a Wisdom (Survival) check to track beasts, you are considered proficient in the Survival skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.
  • Acclimatization (0). You are acclimated to extreme climates and do not suffer the effects of one of the following:
    • Extreme cold below 0 degrees Fahrenheit
    • Extreme heat above 100 degrees Fahrenheit
    • High altitudes above 10,000 ft.
    • Deep waters below 100 ft (n/a if you have swim speed)
  • Survivalist (1). You only need to consume half as much food and drink as a normal creature.

Traversal (1). You can move across difficult terrain without expending extra movement if you are using your walking speed and are on the ground or floor.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Animal Handling, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Stealth, or Survival.

Skilled Tracker (2). Whenever you make a Wisdom (Survival) check to track creatures, you have advantage on that check.

Speech of Beast and Leaf (2). You have the ability to communicate in a limited manner with Beasts, Plants, and vegetation. They can understand the meaning of your words, though you have no special ability to understand them in return. You have advantage on all Charisma checks you make to influence them.

Natural Magic (3). Choose one 1st-level spell from the ranger spell list.

Creativity (6). When making an ability check, attack roll, or saving throw, you can use this trait to gain advantage on the roll. You can choose to do this after the roll has been made but before the result is declared. This trait can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus - 1 per long rest.

Keen Senses (6). You have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or sight.

Theocratic

The society you hail from values devotion to the gods.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Presence (0). You can add 1d4 to any Wisdom (Persuasion) or Strength (Intimidation) checks made against those in your same religion.
  • Blessed (1). Your were blessed with or are able to pray for divine gifts. You can cast guidance on yourself as a reaction a number of times per day equal to your proficiency bonus - 1. You regain uses of this ability at dawn.

Pious Study (1). Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) or Intelligence (Religion) check about religious history, the pantheon of gods, symbolism and other general religious knowledge, you are considered proficient in the History or Religion skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.

Cantrip (2). You know one cantrip from the cleric spell list.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Religion, History, Insight, Persuasion, Deception, Medicine.

Devout Magic (3). Choose one 1st-level spell from the cleric spell list.

Religious Intuition (3). You have advantage on Intelligence (Religion) checks.

Serenity (4). You have advantage on saving throws to avoid or end the charmed or frightened conditions on yourself, and to resist the effects of madness.

Warrior

You were raised in a society dedicated to the arts of war.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one:

  • Combat Training (0). You gain proficiency in 3 simple weapons of your choice.
  • Weaponsmith (0). Your knowledge of weaponry of all kinds and experience in battle gives you the ability to assess your opponent’s equipment. At level 3, if you are observing a creature for one minute, you can assess the properties of a creature’s weapons as if casting identify on them.
    • Combat Climber (0). Your techniques allow you to fight as you climb. You can Climb with a hand occupied. You must still use another hand and both legs to Climb.

Brave (2). You have advantage on saving throws you make to avoid or end the frightened condition on yourself.

Martial Training (2). You gain proficiency in 3 martial weapons of your choice.

Light Armor Training (2). You have proficiency with light armor.

Medium Armor Training (5). You have proficiency with light and medium armor.

Shield Training (3). You have proficiency with shields.

Weapons Expert. Choose one:

  • Savage Attacks (3). When you score a critical hit with a melee weapon attack, you can roll one of the weapon’s damage dice one additional time and add it to the extra damage of the critical hit.
  • Targeted Strike (3). When you score a hit with a melee weapon attack, if you rolled a 19 you can treat the result as a critical hit. This ability can be used a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per long rest.

Ranged Weapon Expert (4). For all weapons of the type you select: gain proficiency with all weapons of that type, ignore the loading property, and firing at long range doesn’t impose disadvantage on your attack roll.

  • Crossbows.
  • Firearms. This can only be taken if firearms exist in your world.

Waterborne

You were raised to feel at home with the sea.

Cultural Skill (required). Choose one or both:

  • Mariner's Lore (0). Whenever you make an Intelligence (History) or Intelligence (Religion) check related to the sea, its creatures and cultures, or water vehicles, you are considered proficient in the History or Religion skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.
  • Fishermen’s Tales (1). Whenever you make an Wisdom (Insight) or Wisdom (Survival) check related to the sea or other bodies of water, you are considered proficient in the Insight or Survival skill and add double your Proficiency Bonus to the check, instead of your normal Proficiency Bonus.

Tool Proficiency (1). You gain proficiency with one tool of your choice: carpenter’s tools, navigator's tools, or vehicles (water).

Weather Worn (1). You do not suffer disadvantage on perception checks due to heavy precipitation and wind, or areas lightly obscured by mist, light fog, or water conditions.

Cantrip (2). You know the gust or mending cantrip.

Swim Speed (2). You have a swim speed of 30 feet provided you are not wearing medium or heavy armor.

Skill Proficiency (2). You have proficiency in one of the following skills of your choice: Acrobatics, Athletics, Perception, Nature, Sleight of Hand, Survival.

Sea Legs (6). You have advantage on Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks, and checks to be knocked prone from effects other than attacks.
